01 · EssencePositioning Biology, engineered for growth
01 Brand essence

We are not a green campaign.
We are an industrial strategy
written in carbon.

Positioning

The BioRevolution Coalition argues that the UK's next industrial chapter is biological. Not a retreat from industry but a redesign of its carbon supply. Plants and microbes as feedstock. Chemistry and policy as the engine. Growth as the outcome.

This is not climate marketing. It is an economic brief, told with the confidence of a white paper and the edge of a campaign.

Audiences

Citizens

Sign the petition. Tone: plainspoken, urgent, practical.

Industry

Join the coalition. Tone: confident, commercial, specific.

Politicians

Champion the policy. Tone: authoritative, evidenced, short.

Tension

Every strong brand holds two ideas in tension. Ours is precision and life. Chemistry and forest. Hexagon and mycelium. The design system makes this tension visible on every page.

Promise

A coalition brief you can hand to a minister, a journalist or a school friend and have it land the same way: this is serious, this is urgent, and this is where the UK should go next.

02 · LogoIdentity mark Primary and mono
02 Logo

One mark. Two surfaces.
No exceptions.

The logo is a hex mark paired with the wordmark. It exists in full colour for light surfaces and in mono for dark. Every other variant is a misuse.

Primary use

Full colour on Cream, Sage-adjacent or light backgrounds. Default for all web, print and press.

Mono use

Cream-on-ink only. Never grey, never black. Reserved for Forest Ink backgrounds and dark hero contexts.

Minimum size

120px wide on screen. 24mm wide in print. Below this, use the hex mark alone.

Clear space

The minimum clear space around the logo equals the height of the hex mark's vertex distance. No copy, image or mark should encroach inside this zone.

Misuse

Correct
Never
  • Do not recolour the hex mark to any non-brand colour.
  • Do not rotate, skew, or italicise the wordmark.
  • Do not place the full colour logo on busy imagery without a solid backing.
  • Do not redraw the hex mark. Use the supplied master files.
  • Do not use the mono logo on Cream. Use the primary.
  • Do not stack the wordmark above the hex. Horizontal lockup only.

Colour rules

  1. 01Body text is always Forest Ink on Cream, or Cream on Forest Ink. Nothing else. This rule is not stylistic, it is accessibility.
  2. 02Leaf Green fails WCAG AA for body text on Cream. Use for CTAs, buttons, progress fill and graphic elements only. Never under 18px type on a light surface.
  3. 03Chartreuse is near-invisible on Cream. Use only on Forest Ink. It is the highlight colour of the dark mode.
  4. 04Amber is decoration. Never CTA, never link, never state. One amber slash at most per section.
  5. 05Sage is for muted copy only. Captions, source lines, form hints. Not primary body.

05 Strapline lockup

A single lockup. Used only for the strapline.

The strapline is set as a two-part lockup. A typographic signature. It appears in the hero, in the closing CTA, and on social cards. It never appears as a generic H1 or headline treatment elsewhere.

06 · MotifVisual system Hex · Voronoi · Mycelium
06 Motif system

Three motifs. Two accents.
Nothing else.

The library is deliberately narrow. Hex as container. Voronoi as precision. Mycelium as growth. Amber and white brushstrokes as rare inflection. Discipline is the visual identity.

Primary motifs

Hexagon

The master container. Six treatments available: solid, outline, double-keyline, pattern-fill, gradient, standalone mark.

Voronoi mesh

The primary pattern fill. Reads as chemistry, molecular structure, precision. Applied inside hex at 8% to 22% opacity.

Mycelium branching

An organic overlay. Bleeds off the canvas edge. Reads as growth, life, biology reclaiming space.

Inflection accents

Use at most once per section. More than twice on a page and the system starts shouting.

Amber slash

A single diagonal stroke. Emphasis only. Never replaces a rule, never an arrow, never a CTA underline.

White brushstrokes

Small hand-drawn scratches. Used only beside pull-quotes, headline numbers or signatures. On Forest Ink only.

Retired motifs

Fractal veins, pinecone spirals and vortex spirals are out of the system as of v2. Do not reintroduce. If more visual variety is required post-launch we will add deliberately, not by accretion.

09 · VoiceTone Authoritative but accessible
09 Voice and tone

Write like a white paper.
Land like a campaign.

We are explaining industrial policy to three audiences at once. Serious, not solemn. Direct, not blunt. Never preachy. Never jargon without translation.

Sentences that sound right

  • Yes"Carbon is a building block of modern life. Today, most of it comes from fossil fuels."
  • Yes"The problem is not a lack of innovation. It is a lack of coherent policy."
  • Yes"Support the transition to bio-based carbon for the future economy."
  • No"Let's save the planet together with the power of biology!" (too campaign, too soft)
  • No"Bio-based polymer feedstock optimisation within a circular industrial matrix." (too jargon, no translation)
10 · WritingHouse style British English · Hyphens only
10 Writing rules

Six rules. Apply every time.

  1. 01British English, always. Colour, organisation, programme, behaviour, realise. Check every draft.
  2. 02Hyphens only. No em-dashes, no en-dashes. If you need a pause, start a new sentence.
  3. 03Numerals carry weight. Spell out one to nine. Use numerals from 10 upward. Always use numerals in stat blocks.
  4. 04Jargon gets a translation. "Bio-based carbon" is followed the first time by "carbon from plants and microbes, not fossil fuels".
  5. 05Cite the source inline where it matters. Never borrow authority without attribution. Source line in caption style, Sage.
  6. 06Short sentences in the hero. Long sentences in the brief. Modulate by context.

12 · PlanWebsite build Single-page advocacy site
12 Website build plan

From system to site,
in five phases.

The brandbook is the contract. The website is the delivery. This is the phased path from this document to a launched petition site, targeting Mon 20 Apr 2026.

  1. P1Foundation. Single HTML file or Next.js app, Tailwind extended with the tokens in Section 03. Self-host Archivo + Archivo Narrow + Inter. Wire up reduced-motion, focus rings, and a lighthouse-safe base. Output: empty page that passes accessibility and renders tokens.
  2. P2Component kit. Build the eight components in Section 08 plus nav, footer, form inputs and the SVG motif library as reusable blocks. Storybook or a simple components page for internal review. Output: a component inventory that matches this brandbook 1:1.
  3. P3Sections in order. Assemble the nine sections from the brief: Hero, Petition, Critical Flaw, Problem, Innovation Gap, Solution, Case Studies, Join, Footer. Alternate Cream and Forest Ink per Section 07. Copy is golden from the campaign master doc; do not rewrite. Output: a scrollable page, no animation yet, content locked.
  4. P4Animation and data. Add the five permitted animations only: hero scroll-morph, Odometer petition count, GSAP DrawSVG on the Solution roadmap, card hover Voronoi, section colour transitions. Wire the petition counter to petition.parliament.uk/petitions/{id}.json via a 15-minute server-side cache. Dynamic OG image via @vercel/og. Output: site feels alive, data is live.
  5. P5Harden and ship. Lighthouse pass: LCP under 2.0s, CLS under 0.1, TBT under 200ms. SVGO all motifs. Staging link for stakeholder review. One copy review, one design review, one technical review. Production deploy, preview-first. Output: live site at T-5.

What is in scope

  • Single-page scrolling site
  • Live petition counter with graceful fallback
  • Three audience CTAs in hero
  • Coalition logo strip in footer (hard-coded at launch)
  • Dynamic OG image with live count

What is out of scope for v1

  • MP champion module
  • News and updates feed
  • Generative signature mosaic
  • Parallax throughout, Three.js hero
  • Second-language versions
  • Coalition member CMS
